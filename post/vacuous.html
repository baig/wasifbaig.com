\(P(a)\) is vacuously true when \(\forall a \in X: P(a)\) and \(X = \emptyset\).

\(P(a)\) is vacuously true when \(\forall a \in X:Q(a) \rightarrow P(a)\) and no \(a \in X\) satisfies \(Q(a)\).

In both cases, it does not matter what statement \(P(a)\) is.
